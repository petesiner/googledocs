Your turn · Google Docs

Creating & collaborating
on a lesson plan

Work through each step to format, share, and collaborate on a lesson plan with your co-teaching team.
By the end, you will
  • Format and structure a document using styles and headings
  • Collaborate with comments and Suggesting mode
  • Insert images and bookmarks for easy navigation
  • Organize and share your doc in Google Drive
0 / 6
01
📄
Create a blank doc & copy the lesson plan
▾
Your team needs a structured lesson plan for an upcoming unit.
  • Open Google Docs and click Blank Document
  • Click "Untitled document" and rename it: [Grade/Subject] Lesson Plan – [Your Name]
  • Copy and paste the lesson plan text into your document
💡
Example title: Grade 6 ELA – Argumentative Writing
✓ Doc created and lesson plan pasted
02
🎨
Format & organize the document
▾
A well-formatted doc makes it easy for co-teachers to navigate quickly.
  • Use the Styles dropdown to set Lesson Objectives, Instructional Activities, and Assessment Plan as Heading 1
  • Number the instructional activities to make each step clear
💡
Headings unlock the document outline panel — helpful for longer docs.
✓ Document structured for easy navigation
03
💬
Add comments & use Suggesting mode
▾
Your co-teacher wants to review and suggest edits without changing the original.
  • Highlight the Writing Task description and click the comment icon
  • Type: "Should we add a sentence starter here?" and submit
  • Go to View → Mode → Suggesting and try editing a sentence
💡
Suggestions appear in a distinct color and can be accepted or rejected by any editor.
✓ Feedback tracked and visible
04
🔗
Insert a bookmark & hyperlink
▾
Make the doc self-navigating so readers can jump to key sections.
  • Insert a page break before the assessment section and add the heading Lesson Assessments
  • Change the font to blue, size 24, and choose a different style
  • Insert a 2×4 table labeled Assessment Type and Link
  • Click before "Lesson Assessments" → Insert → Bookmark
  • Scroll up, highlight "Assessment Plan" → Insert → Link → choose the bookmark
💡
Bookmarks + links work like a mini table of contents inside your doc.
✓ Readers can now jump directly to assessments
05
🖼️
Insert an image
▾
Visuals help anchor the document and give it a polished feel.
  • Click Insert → Image → Search the Web
  • Search for Research Paper, insert an image, and move it to the top of the page
💡
Click the image once inserted to adjust wrap settings — inline, wrap text, etc.
✓ Image inserted and positioned
06
📁
Share & organize in Google Drive
▾
Your co-teaching team needs access to collaborate in real time.
  • Click the folder icon next to the doc title → New Folder
  • Name it Lesson Plans & Resources, click Create, then Move Here
  • Click Share, enter demoteachergce@gmail.com, set as Editor, and click Send
💡
Organizing before sharing keeps everything findable for the whole team.
✓ Lesson plan organized and shared
Self-assessment checklist
  • Doc created, titled, and formatted
  • Lesson plan copied and organized
  • Comments and suggestions added
  • Bookmark and hyperlink working
  • Image inserted and positioned
  • Doc shared and stored in Drive
